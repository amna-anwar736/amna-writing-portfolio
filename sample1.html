5 SEO Mistakes That Are Killing Your Website Traffic

Most businesses spend thousands building a beautiful website — then wonder why nobody visits it. The answer is almost always one of these five mistakes.

✍️ Amna Anwar 📅 2025 ⏱️ 5 min read 🏷️ SEO · Digital Marketing

Search Engine Optimization is one of those topics that everyone has heard of but very few people actually get right. After working with businesses on their digital presence, I've noticed the same mistakes coming up again and again — mistakes that are completely avoidable once you know what to look for.

Here are the 5 most common SEO mistakes I see, and exactly how to fix them.

Mistake 01

Targeting Keywords Nobody Searches For

This is the most common mistake by far. People write content around keywords they think people are searching — but never actually verify the search volume. You could write a perfect 2,000-word article and get zero traffic because the keyword gets 10 searches per month.

The fix: Before writing anything, use free tools like Google Keyword Planner or Ubersuggest to check actual search volume. Target keywords with decent volume (500+ monthly searches) and low-to-medium competition, especially if your site is new.

💡 Pro Tip

Long-tail keywords (3–5 words) are easier to rank for and often have higher conversion intent. "Best WordPress theme for small business" converts better than just "WordPress theme."

Mistake 02

Ignoring Meta Descriptions

Your meta description is the short text that appears under your page title in Google search results. Most people either leave it blank or write something generic like "Welcome to our website." This is a massive missed opportunity.

The fix: Write a compelling meta description (150–160 characters) for every page. Include your primary keyword naturally and give the reader a reason to click. Think of it as a mini ad for your page.

Bad meta description: "We offer digital marketing services for businesses."

Good meta description: "Struggling to rank on Google? Our SEO strategies have helped 50+ businesses double their organic traffic in 90 days. Let's talk."

Mistake 03

Slow Website Speed Is Silently Hurting You

Google officially uses page speed as a ranking factor. A website that takes more than 3 seconds to load loses over 50% of its visitors before they even see the content. Worse, Google deprioritizes slow sites in search results.

The fix: Run your site through Google PageSpeed Insights (free). The most common culprits are:

  • Uncompressed images (use tools like Squoosh.app)
  • Too many plugins on WordPress
  • No caching plugin installed
  • Cheap or shared hosting with slow servers
Mistake 04

No Internal Linking Strategy

Internal links — links that connect one page of your website to another — are one of the easiest SEO wins that most people completely ignore. They help Google understand the structure of your site, distribute page authority, and keep visitors on your site longer.

The fix: Every blog post or page should link to at least 2–3 other relevant pages on your site. When you mention a topic you've covered elsewhere, link to it. It's that simple — and it adds up fast.

Mistake 05

Writing for Search Engines Instead of Humans

Stuffing keywords into every sentence used to work in 2012. Today, Google's algorithm is sophisticated enough to penalize this. More importantly, real humans find it unreadable — and if people bounce off your page immediately, that signals to Google that your content isn't valuable.

The fix: Write for your reader first. Use your keyword naturally — in the title, first paragraph, a few subheadings, and the conclusion. Focus on answering the question your reader came with. Google rewards content that genuinely helps people.

SEO Doesn't Have to Be Complicated

Fix these 5 mistakes and you'll already be ahead of most websites in your niche. SEO is a long game — but these are the foundations everything else is built on.
